\section{Introduction}
Sudoku is one of those puzzles that looks deceptively simple: fill an $n \times n$ grid with the digits $1$ through $n$ so that no row, column, or subgrid repeats a value. Behind that simplicity, though, lies a rich constraint satisfaction problem, which is why it has become such a popular testbed in the optimization literature~\cite{lloyd2020,simonis2005sudoku,manyam2024,SSAS}. The earliest solvers leaned on deterministic backtracking~\cite{norvig2006solving,Schott,bhattarai2025study,is2012techniques} or tried to mimic the pencil-and-paper strategies that human players use~\cite{crook2009pencil,bhattarai2025study}. Both work fine for small grids, but they hit a wall once the puzzle dimensions start growing. That limitation motivated a wave of swarm-based metaheuristics---Artificial Bee Colony~\cite{pacurib2009}, Particle Swarm Optimization~\cite{PSO,GPSO}, Ant Colony Optimization (ACO)~\cite{lloyd2020}---which are better equipped to navigate large search spaces. Among these, ACO stood out as especially promising, particularly when paired with constraint propagation (CP) to prune infeasible regions early~\cite{norvig2006solving,lloyd2020}.

Still, there is a persistent gap in the literature. Most solvers lean into either constraint-aware search or parallel exploration, but rarely both at once. Hybrid CP--ACO methods do a good job of shrinking the feasible space, yet they typically run a single colony on a single thread, which makes them prone to pheromone stagnation as grids get larger~\cite{lloyd2020}. Multi-colony designs like DCM-ACO address part of this by using entropy-based cooperation across heterogeneous colonies to keep a healthy tension between convergence and diversity---but they still operate within one processing context, with no way to coordinate across threads~\cite{mo2022}. On the parallel side, approaches such as RMACO spread colonies over multiple processors using structured communication topologies, but they skip over the question of colony-role heterogeneity and do not actively regulate diversity within each colony~\cite{yang2015}. The upshot is that none of these methods reliably sustain both convergence and diversity as puzzle complexity scales up.

This paper introduces \textbf{SudoSLVRR} to bridge that gap. SudoSLVRR is a multithreaded Sudoku solver that nests DCM-ACO inside a communication-aware parallel architecture. Each thread hosts a full complement of heterogeneous colonies; internally, their pheromone landscapes are coordinated through entropy-regulated cooperation, while externally, threads exchange solutions via ring-topology and random-matching protocols. The result is a solver that can balance exploration and exploitation within each thread and, at the same time, share useful information across threads---yielding robust performance on $9 \times 9$, $16 \times 16$, and $25 \times 25$ puzzles.
