Lloyd and Amos~\cite{lloyd2020} pair constraint propagation with Ant Colony System (ACS)~\cite{dorigo2004ant}: CP first strips out invalid cell-value assignments, and then ants build solutions steered by a global pheromone matrix. To keep the pheromone landscape from going stale, they introduce a Best Value Evaporation (BVE) operator that gradually decays deposited pheromone over time. The approach works well up to a point, but because it relies on a single colony, the convergence it gains tends to come at the cost of search diversity---a trade-off that becomes increasingly painful on harder, larger puzzles.

Mo et al.~\cite{mo2022} tackle that diversity problem head-on with DCM-ACO, a heterogeneous framework that runs two ACS sub-colonies alongside one Max--Min Ant System (MMAS)~\cite{dorigo2004ant} sub-colony. The key idea is a cooperative game strategy that redistributes pheromone income across the ACS colonies based on both solution quality and information entropy. When an ACS colony starts to stagnate, a pheromone fusion mechanism kicks in, blending its matrix with the MMAS colony's pheromone landscape to inject fresh information. On top of that, a public path recommendation feeds commonly selected assignments from the ACS colonies into MMAS, giving it a convergence boost. The net effect is a nice balance between fast exploitation through ACS and broad exploration through MMAS. The catch, however, is that DCM-ACO runs entirely within a single process---there is simply no mechanism for spreading the computation across multiple threads.

Yang and Fang~\cite{yang2015} come at the problem from the parallelism angle with RMACO, a parallel ACO distributed over MPI where each processor hosts its own colony. Colonies share information through a dual topology: a fixed ring passes along iteration-best solutions, while a randomly matched partner supplies best-so-far solutions. The exchange schedule is adaptive---longer intervals early on let each colony explore independently, then shorter intervals later help the whole system zero in on good regions faster. This three-source pheromone update gives each colony a broader view of the search landscape, but RMACO uses homogeneous colonies throughout and has no entropy-driven mechanism for regulating diversity. It is worth noting that earlier parallel designs had their own shortcomings: Parallel Independent Runs and fine-grained ant-level parallelism~\cite{randall2002} suffer from redundant exploration due to absent information sharing and synchronization overhead on global pheromone structures, respectively.

\textbf{SudoSLVRR} draws on all three of these threads. It brings together CP-guided ACO search, heterogeneous multi-colony cooperation with entropy-based pheromone regulation, and structured dual-topology communication---all within a multithreaded framework designed to handle Sudoku puzzles of varying sizes.
